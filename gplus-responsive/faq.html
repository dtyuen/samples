Assets & Guidelines FAQs

Here are answers to some of the most popular questions we get.

Can I use the full color Google wordmark?

We reserve the full color wordmark for communications from Google only.

Why does Google+ need a toolkit to outline how to use its brand assets?

These guidelines are intended to promote consistent use of the Google+ brand. This prevents consumer confusion, makes it easier for people to instantly recognize references to Google+, and helps make Google+ a stronger brand and a more valuable partner for your marketing efforts. These guidelines also help protect company trademarks.

Can I combine Google+ brand assets with new terms or artwork to create my own marks an logos, or modify the color or design to fit with the style of my website or packaging?

If you're unable to use the correct color due to technical limitations, please use black and white. You may scale the size of brand assets to suit your needs, as long as they are not the most distinctive or prominent feature on your web page, printed material, or other content. You may not manipulate or alter the Google+ Logo or assets in any way.

Can I use Google+ brand assets in my advertisement, play, film or book?

You can use Google+ in messaging as long as it is clear that Google+ doesn't endorse or sponsor your project. You may not use Google+ logos or images in any publication without permission.

Can I use Google+ brand assets I found elsewhere on the web?

Please only use the official brand assets that are available from this site. Assets downloaded from the web are not guaranteed to be current.
