How to use Google+ in your marketing. It's all here.

Thanks for using Google+. To help you make the most of it, we created this set of guidelines to show you how you can use the Google+ name, logos, icons, and language in your marketing.

In addition to brand assets you can download and use in your materials, we've included a few examples to show you how it all comes together nicely.

Where to use
the Wordmark

Integrating the wordmark in your materials is easy. Here are a few dos and don'ts to follow that will make it seamless.

 
Do

In most cases, the grey wordmark should be used on white. The white mark can be reversed out of dark or bright colors.

 
Don't

Apply the white wordmark to light colors, nor the grey wordmark to dark colors. Never re-color the wordmark.

Use the full-color wordmark.

Apply the wordmark to patterns, textures and photographic backgrounds that are overly busy.

How to use the Wordmark in copy

It's always a great idea to promote any of your activities taking place on Google+. We recommend using a 'run-up' line to guide people there.

 
Do

Guide people to your activity, using "on" directly before the wordmark.

See the examples below for tips on spacing when using the logo in your run-up line.

The wordmark should only appear at the end of a sentence.

 
Don't

Imply that we are a sponsor, co-creator, or promoter of your brand.

Use Google or Google+ as a verb. As in, "Just Google+ it."

How to use the
Google+ icon

This is the Google+ icon. It's used when the wordmark (preferred) won't work with your design. Our favorite is the red on white, but it can also be reversed out of any color using the grey or white Google+ icon.

 
Do

Always select a color that provides contrast to your artwork.

You can apply the Google+ icon to patterns, textures and photographic backgrounds, but not if they are overly busy.

Customize the Google+ icon when grouped with other third-party social icons in a way that accommodates your site design, provided that any styling in size, shape, or color is consistent as to create a uniform appearance.

Always center the g+ in the icon

Preserve the aspect ratio

 
Don't

Apply the Google+ icon to colors that are similar.

Change the font of the "g" or the position of the "+" symbol in the icon

How to use the
Google+ icon in copy

You can use the Google+ icon in a 'run-up' line, just like you would with the wordmark, when promoting activities happening specifically on Google+. We recommend these dos and don'ts when crafting your message.

 
Do

Guide people to your activity, using "on" directly before the Google+ icon.

Write your line so the Google+ icon ends the statement.
